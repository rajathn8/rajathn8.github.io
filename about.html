My Story

From Bangalore to London - A journey of code and curiosity

I started my journey in tech back in 2017, fresh out of Visvesvaraya Technological University with an engineering degree and an insatiable curiosity for how things work. My first role at IBM introduced me to the world of data analytics and automation - and I was hooked.

Over the years, I've had the privilege of working with some incredible teams. At NextOrbit, I built data pipelines that processed gigabytes of data in near real-time. At Infoblox, I dove deep into network security, building microservices that monitor DNS performance across enterprise networks.

The defining chapter of my career came at Goldman Sachs, where I spent over three years as a Vice President, building and optimizing high-performance Python microservices on AWS. I led system migrations, built monitoring solutions, and learned what it truly means to build systems that handle mission-critical financial operations.

"The best code is the code that doesn't exist - simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."

Today, I'm at Intropic, working at the exciting intersection of AI and financial analytics. I'm passionate about leveraging Generative AI to build smarter, more intuitive systems - and I believe we're just scratching the surface of what's possible.

Outside of work, I'm an avid traveler and adventurer. I've trekked to Everest Base Camp, explored the ancient city of Petra, camped under the stars in Wadi Rum, and caught waves on beaches around the world. These experiences remind me that the best solutions often come when we step away from the screen and see the world from a different perspective.
